information that identifies you, either alone or in combination with other data.) We also collect information that is non-personal and does not, on its own, identify you.
When non-personal information is combined with other information so that it does identify you, we treat that combination as personal information.

<ul>

Once you register with us and sign in to our services, you are not anonymous to us.
</p> Billing Information <p> While registration is free, if you choose to purchase certain additional services and products, we may collect the following personal information: </p>
<ul>
<li>Credit card or other billing account information</li>
<li>Billing address</li>
<li>Transaction-related information</li>
<li>Fraud prevention information (see Fraud and Abuse Prevention)</li>

</p>
<ul>
<li>When you search for a person using our website, we collect a log of your searches.</li>
<li>If you wish to enhance your search results using information from third-party services such as Facebook or LinkedIn, we may ask you to give us authorization to access information about you stored in those third-party services.</li>
<li> To use our identity protection products, we may ask for the following personal information: <ul>

<li>The browser and operating system you are using to access our website</li>

We will never ask you to give us your password for a third-party service.
</p> Social Media Widgets <p> Our website includes social media features, such as the Facebook Like button and Widgets, such as the Share This button or interactive mini-programs that run on our site.
These features may collect your IP address, which page you are visiting on our site, and may set a cookie to enable the feature to function properly.
Social media features and Widgets are either hosted by a third party or hosted directly on our Site.
Your interactions with these Features are governed by the privacy policy of the company providing it.
</p> Cookies and Other Tracking Technologies <p> We use several methods to collect technical and analytics data, including the following: </p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Cookies</strong>.
We may set and access our cookies on your computer.
peoplesmart.com uses both session cookies and persistent cookies.
A session cookie expires when you close your browser.
We use session cookies to make it easier for you to navigate the site and log in to your account.
A persistent cookie remains on your hard drive for an extended period of time.
We use persistent cookies to track usage of our site to improve your experience, and to remember your preferences.
You can remove persistent cookies by following directions provided in your Internet browser’s “help” directory. Learn more about cookies.
You may disable cookies in your browser settings, but your ability to use some areas of our site will be limited.
</li>
<li>
<strong>Third-Party Cookies</strong>.
The use of cookies by our partners, affiliates, and service providers is not covered by our privacy policy.
We do not have access or control over these cookies.
Our partners, affiliates, and service providers use session and persistent cookies to make it easier for you to navigate our site, monitor site usage and performance, and provide targeted ads.
</li>
<li>
<strong>Web Beacons</strong>.
Our third party partners employ clear gifs (a.k.a.
Web Beacons/Web Bugs), images, and scripts that help them better manage content on our site.
We do not tie the information gathered to our Customers’ or Users’ personally identifiable information.
</li>
<li>
<strong>Flash Cookies</strong>.
We use Flash cookies (also known as local storage objects or LSOs) to store content information and preferences.
Third parties, with whom we partner to provide certain features on our site, prevent fraud, or to display advertising based upon your Web browsing activity, may use LSOs to collect and store information.
Various browsers may offer their own management tools for removing HTML5 LSOs.
To manage Flash LSOs please click here.
</li>
<li>
<strong>Log Data</strong>.
Your browser automatically sends certain data that is recorded by our servers every time you interact with this website, including your IP address, browser type, operating system, the referring web page, pages visited, location, your mobile carrier, device and application IDs, and search terms.
</li>

<ul>

<li>Respond to your comments and questions and provide customer service</li>
<li>Provide and deliver the products and services that you request</li>
<li>Conduct research to improve the quality of our products and services</li>

<li>Protect our users against fraud and abuse</li>
<li>Communicate with you about new products and services offered by Inflection and our selected partners</li>
</ul>How do we protect your information? Technical and Administrative Controls <ul>
<li>Your information is stored on servers in physically secure locations</li>
<li>We implement technical measures to prevent unauthorized access, and keep security patches and software up-to-date</li>
<li>Employee access to your information is restricted and audited</li>
<li>Sensitive personal information is stored in encrypted form</li>
<li>All personal information is encrypted during transfer (for example, when providing credit card information)</li>
</ul>Payment Information <p> For processing payments, we use systems that are compliant with the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS).

for example, when you use a third-party web client to access your account, or when you give us permission to make information public.
</p> Within the Inflection Family of Products <p> Other Inflection products may use your information, but only in ways that are consistent with this Privacy Policy.
</p> To Provide Certain Services <p> We use certain trusted third parties to provide certain services for us.

In addition, any third-party service provider we use is required to give your information the same protections that are outlined here in this Privacy Policy.
</p> Public, Anonymous and Aggregated Data <p> We may share or disclose information that is non-private, anonymously aggregated, or otherwise non-personal.

The obligations of this Privacy Policy will continue to apply to the new entity after the sale or transfer.

</p>
<p> When we detect suspected fraud or abuse, we may share information associated with that particular transaction or event (including, but not limited to, payment information, email addresses, and unique device identifiers) with our security partners or law enforcement for further investigation.
</p> Legal Compliance and Harm Prevention <p> We may preserve or share your information in the following circumstances: </p>
<ul>
<li>When required by law, regulation, or legal request such as a warrant or subpoena</li>
<li>When we believe it is reasonably necessary in urgent situations to protect the well-being or safety of any individual</li>
<li>To address fraud, security, or technical issues</li>
<li>To protect Inflection’s rights or property</li>
</ul>
