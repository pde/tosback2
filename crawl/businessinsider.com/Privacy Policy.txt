<strong>
<strong>Overview</strong>
</strong>
</p>
<p>This privacy policy applies to Business Insider (www.businessinsider.com) on all platforms (web, mobile web, apps, emails).</p>
<p>Business Insider contains links to other sites and we are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of such other sites.
We encourage our users to be aware when they leave our site and to read the privacy statements of each and every Web site that collects Personally Identifiable Information (PII).</p>
<p>Business Insider takes privacy concerns seriously.
This privacy policy outlines what information is collected when you visit our site. </p>
<p>Business Insider gathers information about its users mainly to enhance their experience on the site and on email newsletters.
This information is used to bring you content that is relevant to your interests.
Additionally, Business Insider, along with our advertising partners, gather information about you anonymously to provide you with more targeted advertising messages that are catered to your location and interests.
You can opt out of information gathering for advertising purposes below.</p>
<p>
<strong>Information Collected</strong>
</p>
<p>
<em>
<strong>Personal Information/ Information You Directly Provide To Business Insider (BI):</strong>
</em>
</p>
<p>When registering, commenting, or signing up for newsletters, you may provide Business Insider with personal information, including but not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Personal contact information (name, zip code, e-mail address, phone numbers)</li>
<li>Comments</li>
<li>User names (including social media user names)</li>
<li>Correspondence with Business Insider staff</li>
<li>Billing Information (for paid Business Insider products)</li>
</ul>
<p>You are not required to provide Business Insider with any personal information in order to use the site.
All information that is collected is the sole property of the site, and that information is not distributed outside of the site unless required by law.</p>
<p>
<strong>
<em>Non-Personal Information Automatically Collected</em>
</strong>
</p>
<p>This type of information is collected anonymously through the use of tracking technology using third-party cookies, web beacons, and other similar methods.
No Personally Identifiable Information (PII) is collected through this methodology.
 This information may include, but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Hardware/software information (geographic location, IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, bandwidth, etc.)</li>
<li>Analytics information (time spent on site, number of pages viewed, referring web page, browsing information, etc.)</li>
<li>Advertising information (type of ads seen, number of times ads were served, pages where the ads were served)</li>
<li>Information collected through HTML cookies, web beacons, and similar technologies.</li>
</ul>
<p>
<em>
<strong>Information Collected Through Third-Party Sources</strong>
</em>
</p>
<p>This type of information is collected anonymously by approved third-party partners (such as online marketing firms, research companies, social media companies, ad targeting firms, and other partners).
None of the information collected by these sources contains any PII.
This type of information includes but is not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Demographic information (gender, age, HHI, job title, interests, etc.)</li>
<li>Advertising information (clicks, interactions, post-impression performance data)</li>
<li>Social networking information (Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, etc.)</li>
<li>Research information (data collected from partners such as Nielsen, Comscore, Quantcast, etc.)</li>
</ul>
<p>
<strong>What Is Done With The Collected Information?</strong>
</p>
<p>
<em>Site Content</em>: Business Insider partners with Sailthru to provide you with content that is relevant and personalized to your reading interests.
You can read Sailthru's privacy policy here.</p>
<p>
<em>Email Communications</em>: Business Insider may use the information that you submit to send you email communications, such as newsletters, breaking news, information about the site, alerts, and promotional messages from advertising partners.</p>
<p>
<em>Site Analysis</em>: Business Insider uses the information collected through third-party sources to learn and analyze how the site is used.
We use this information to enhance the site by making improvements based on the data collected.</p>
<p>
<em>Advertising</em>: Display Ads are delivered from our adserver, Open AdSream, powered by WPP’s Real Media Group.
 At times third-party information is used to deliver targeted ads to you, your location, and your interests.
We also use Third-Party Ad Platforms (such as Google AdSense and Pubmatic) to deliver ads on our site.
No PII is exchanged with any type of advertising message on Business Insider.</p>
<p>

</p>
<p>There are very few specific circumstances when your information is shared outside of the company, these circumstances include but are not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li>If you comment or use the Facebook app on Business Insider, the information you submit (including your social media user name, etc) is viewable to the public. </li>
<li>
<p>Business Insider may need to disclose personal information to comply with a judicial proceeding, a court order, or any other legal process.</p>
</li>
<li>We employ third-party services to distribute email newsletters and these third-parties have access to specific information only for the purposes of fulfilling contracts on our behalf.<br> </li>
<li>
In the event Business Insider goes through a business transition, such as a merger, acquisition by another company, or sale of a portion of its assets, users' personal information will, in most instances, be part of the assets transferred.</li>
</ul>
